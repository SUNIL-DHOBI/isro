ARYABHATA

The Aryabhata satellite was India's first unmanned satellite, launched on April 19, 1975, from a Soviet launch site. Named after the ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer, it was designed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to conduct experiments in solar physics and X-ray astronomy. Although a power failure cut short its experimental phase, the satellite successfully transmitted data and marked a significant milestone, establishing India's presence in space
