ISRO - India's Space Journy

1969: Birth of ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organization is establish, making the begining of India's space program

1975: Aryabhata

India launched its first satellite, Aryabhata, marking its entry into space exploration.

2008: Chandrayaan-1

India's first lunar mission successfully orbited the Moon.

2014: Mangalyaan

The Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) successfully entered Mars orbit on its first attempt, a global first.

2023: Chandrayaan-3

India achieved a soft landing on the Moon, becoming the fourth nation to do so.



ISRO - Mission Highlights
Lunar Exploration

Chandrayaan-3

ISRO successfully lands near the Moon's south pole, a historic achievement in lunar exploration. The mission's objectives included studying the lunar surface and environment.

Human Space Flight

Gaganyaan

ISRO's ambitious Gaganyaan program aims to demonstrate India's capability to send humans to low Earth orbit and bring them back safely, a major step towards crewed missions.

Solar Mission

Aditya-L1

ISRO's first solar mission, Aditya-L1, is designed to study the Sun's atmosphere from the Sun-Earth Lagrange point L1. providing continuous observation of our star.
