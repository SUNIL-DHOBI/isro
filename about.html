About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation is the national space agency of India,
pioneering space exploration and technology for national development.


Our Journey

The journey of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) began in 1969 with a vision to develop space technology and apply it for national development. Starting with limited resources and humble beginnings, ISRO achieved its first milestone by launching India's first satellite, Aryabhata, in 1975. Over the decades, ISRO has grown into a premier space agency recognized globally for its innovative and cost-effective space missions.
br Major achievements include the successful launch of the Indian Remote Sensing (IRS) satellites, the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle families, and landmark interplanetary missions like Chandrayaan for lunar exploration and Mangalyaan for Mars.

Our Mission

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is dedicated to harnessing the potential of space technology for the advancement and development of the nation. Its mission encompasses designing, building, and launching satellites and spacecraft that serve various purposes, including telecommunications, earth observation, weather forecasting, and scientific exploration. ISRO strives to contribute to the socio-economic progress of India by developing space-based applications that enhance communication, navigation, agriculture, education, and disaster management, while constantly pushing the boundaries of space science and planetary exploration.

Our Vision

The mission of the Indian Space Research Organisation is to harness space technology and its applications for national development, while conducting space science research and planetary exploration to expand knowledge. ISRO focuses on developing advanced satellite systems, launch vehicles, and space-based services that benefit sectors such as telecommunications, weather forecasting, agriculture, education & disaster management.The organization envisions empowering India with world-class infrastructure in space science and technology, while playing a pivotal role in enhancing humanity's understanding of the universe for the betterment of society.


Key Achivements - ISRO


Chandrayaan - 3

Successful soft landing on the lunar south pole, making india the fourth country to achieve this feat and the first to land on the lunar south pole region.

Mars Orbiter Mission

Made India the first Asian nation to reach Martian orbit and the first nation in the world to do so in its maiden attempt.

NavIC Constellation

An independent regional navigation satellite system that provides accurate real-time positioning and timing services.
